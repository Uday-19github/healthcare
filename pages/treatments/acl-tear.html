ACL Reconstruction Surgery

ACL (Anterior Cruciate Ligament) reconstruction restores knee stability after a tear, common in sports injuries. Arthroscopic surgery uses your own tissue to rebuild the ligament.

Symptoms

Symptoms of ACL Tear

If you experience any of the following symptoms, consult a specialist promptly:

  • ⊕Loud pop at time of injury
  • ⊕Severe pain and inability to continue activity
  • ⊕Rapid swelling
  • ⊕Loss of range of motion
  • ⊕Knee giving way during activity
Causes

What Causes It?

Understanding the underlying causes helps in prevention and treatment:

  • ⊕Sports injuries (football, basketball, skiing)
  • ⊕Sudden direction changes
  • ⊕Awkward landing from a jump
  • ⊕Direct contact or collision
Procedure

How the Procedure Works

Arthroscopic reconstruction uses a graft (from hamstring or patellar tendon) to replace the torn ACL. The graft is anchored with tiny screws. Surgery takes 1–1.5 hours.
